Diphthongs “ee” and “ea”


A diphthong is two vowel sounds that combine to make one vowel sound.

The "ee" and "ea" sounds are like magic pairs of letters that love to be together. When "ee" and "ea" come along, they make a long "eee" sound, just like the sound you hear when you say "tree" or "leaf."

Remember, "ee" and "ea" are vowel buddies! They love to make words sound sweet and long, just like the words "see," "meet," "tea," and "beach."

Remember, when you see "ee" or "ea" in a word, they often make the same sound.

But sometimes in the diphthong “ea”, words like "fear," “tear,” “clear” can be a little different.

"ee" Words:

  • Bee - The bee buzzes around the flowers.
  • Tree - The tree is very tall.
  • Feet - My feet are in the sand.
  • See - I can see the stars at night.
  • Sleep - It’s time to go to sleep.

"ea" Words:

  • Leaf - The leaf is green.
  • Tea - My mom drinks tea in the morning.
  • Read - I love to read books.
  • Bean - The bean is small and round.
  • Sea - We went to the sea to swim.
